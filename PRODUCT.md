# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Photographers and photo-minded iPhone owners — people who already shoot Live Photos and
video and wish they could get tripod-and-DSLR long-exposure results without the gear. They
land on this page deciding whether the app earns a download. Their context: evaluating, often
skeptical of "AI photo magic," and able to tell a real photographic result from a filter.

## Product Purpose

Marketing landing page for **Long Exposures**, an iOS app that turns Live Photos and videos
into long-exposure photographs where *you* pick which frames blend. The page exists to convert
a visitor into an App Store download by proving the app produces real, controllable results
(silky water, light trails, motion blur), and by making the on-device / no-upload privacy
stance plain. Success = the visitor believes the output is real and taps the App Store badge.

## Brand Personality

Precise, tactile, instrument-grade. The voice of a well-made camera, not a startup. Three
words: **precise, tactile, confident.** It should feel like a piece of photographic equipment
— a darkroom enlarger, a machined exposure dial — that happens to live on a phone. Quietly
expert; lets the photographs and the controls do the talking. Never hypey, never "magic."

## Anti-references

- Generic glassy dark-SaaS landing pages (blurred glass cards, mesh-gradient orbs, tiny
  uppercase eyebrow above every heading, gradient-clip headline text). The first draft of this
  page leaned here; the redesign moves off it deliberately.
- Filter-app / consumer-cute aesthetics (rounded pastel everything, sticker UI).
- Editorial-magazine cosplay (display-serif + italic drop caps + broadsheet rules) — wrong
  register for a precision tool.
- Anything that reads "AI made that."

## Design Principles

1. **Show the output.** This is a photography product; real long-exposure photographs carry
   the page. Copy and UI recede behind the image. (Imagery is user-supplied; placeholders are
   explicitly marked, never disguised as finished art.)
2. **The interface IS the proof.** The app's real superpower is *you pick the frames*. Make the
   timeline/range control an interactive, instrument-grade hero element, not a screenshot.
3. **Instrument, not startup.** Tactile, machined, restrained. Technical numerals and precise
   spacing over decorative flourish. Motion is mechanical and purposeful, never bouncy.
4. **Privacy is a feature, stated plainly.** On-device, nothing uploaded — said once, with
   conviction, not as a badge wall.
5. **Earn every accent.** True-dark surface; the icon's electric blue is a functional signal
   (active frames, the live readout), used sparingly so it means something.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI text ≥3:1 against the true-dark surface.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support — the mechanical motion has crossfade/instant fallbacks;
  content is never gated behind a reveal animation.
- Interactive controls keyboard-operable and labeled; the blue accent is never the sole carrier
  of meaning (paired with position/shape).
